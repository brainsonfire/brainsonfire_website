Backdrop

When your main competitor has an overwhelming stronghold on the market—and a traditional advertising budget to match—it forces you to start thinking outside the box. In what is often considered an unemotional category, Liquid Wrench saw opportunity. They knew that behind every DIY project there is a passion, a person and a story. In order to expand into the growing “maker” space, however, Liquid Wrench realized they would need educate tinker-minded consumers about why using their specially formulated lubricants, rather than the competition’s single-formula solution, was the right choice. And they’d have to do it on a budget.

Purpose

Establish Liquid Wrench as relevant in the cultural conversation about making, restoring, fixing and tinkering. Help the brand become a part of popular culture as a supportive partner to DIYers. Increase online and offline brand-related conversation. Enhance consumer engagement with the brand through social media. Increase positive sentiment and brand loyalty. Foster a relationship between Liquid Wrench and the Maker Movement.

Action

Brains on Fire laid the foundation for a content-driven community called Tinkernation, powered by Liquid Wrench. The site features a range of nostalgic and inspirational maker-relevant content including stories, snippets and slices of life featuring the people behind the projects. During the insight phase of our engagement with Liquid Wrench it became apparent that the space was flooded with “how-to” sites. Rather than reinventing the wheel, Tinkernation takes a different approach by celebrating the “why” that drives people from all walks of life to make, restore, fix and tinker.

The Tinkernation community came out in full force in June of 2013 to honor of the greatest tinkerers of all, dads everywhere. The Pop’s Wisdom Project encouraged the Tinkernation community to submit tidbits of fatherly advice that had helped them get “unstuck” when facing life’s more challenging moments. Within the first few hours after the program went live, Tinkernation fans had submitted over 600 impassioned pearls of wisdom from their Pops.

We’re still in the early innings of building and growing the Tinkernation community. Having said that, in the short time since launch, Liquid Wrench has continued implementing new and innovative ways to genuinely connect with DIYers and Makers by celebrating their insatiable urge to make something…and in the process, make something of themselves. Check back for what is sure to be more good stuff coming soon from Tinkernation and Liquid Wrench.
